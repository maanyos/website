Computer Engineering

NUS Computer Engineering is a multidisciplinary program that combines the principles of computer science and electrical engineering. The program covers a wide range of topics, from physical electronic hardware to software programming, with a focus on embedded systems, circuit design, and low-level programming. Many courses involve group projects where students use robotics to perform specific tasks, by developing the necessary electrical circuitry and writing the code that enables the robots to complete these tasks.

Innovation & Design Program

The Innovation & Design Program (iDP) aims to cultivate an entrepreneurial mindset among students, emphasizing innovation and problem-solving skills. Students learn to design new products, services, and experiences that address real customer needs. Through hands-on, major design projects with real-world impact, students collaborate across disciplines to tackle challenges in areas like healthcare, mobility, sustainability and more. Equipped with valuable skills in design thinking, innovation, and entrepreneurship, students are prepared to tackle complex societal issues and pursue technopreneurial ventures.

University Scholar's Program

The University Scholars Program (USP) is dedicated to nurturing independent and adaptable thinkers who will positively impact the world. Through transformative learning environments in small seminars, students explore diverse intellectual interests, transcending disciplinary boundaries and embracing new perspectives. USP expands students' worldviews, empowering them to challenge conventions and develop bold, actionable ideas. Anchored in pillars of curiosity, critical thinking, courage, and engagement, USP students acquire the confidence and skills to address significant global challenges.
